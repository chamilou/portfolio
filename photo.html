Diaphragm

Diaphragm or in other words "F-STOP" or "F-Number" or "Aperture" is simply how your camera handling the quantity of light coming through your linses. Diaphragm affect 2 things in your picture: first is light coming in, so brightness, and second is so called "depth of field". Depth of filed is what is the distance which is in focus. Smaller is the hole larger is the depth of field, but lower is the light too! It is mesured 1/number, 1/1 being the largest number! With the diaphragm whide open so "f/1.8" or "f/2" you get the "Bokeh" effects. To note that linses which offer higher opening are more costly
